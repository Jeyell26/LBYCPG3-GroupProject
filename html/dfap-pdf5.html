ECE/CPE Research Ethics Policy

Introduction

As members of the Electrical and Computer Engineering (ECE) and Computer Engineering (CPE) programs at De La Salle University, we are committed to upholding the highest standards of research ethics. Research ethics is integral to the pursuit of knowledge and the responsible conduct of research in our academic community.

Policy Statement

ECE/CPE students and faculty are expected to conduct research with the utmost integrity, transparency, and respect for human subjects, data, and intellectual property. The following principles govern research ethics in our department:

Respect for Human Subjects

All research involving human subjects must comply with the principles of informed consent, privacy, confidentiality, and protection of participants' rights and welfare. Researchers must obtain proper informed consent from participants before their involvement in any research study. Additionally, researchers should respect the privacy and confidentiality of participants' personal information and ensure that the data collected is used only for research purposes and reported in an anonymized manner when possible.

Integrity in Data Collection and Reporting

Researchers must maintain the highest standards of integrity in data collection, analysis, and reporting. All research data should be recorded accurately and truthfully, and any manipulation or falsification of data is strictly prohibited. Researchers should disclose any potential conflicts of interest that may arise during the research process and ensure that their findings are reported in an unbiased and transparent manner.

Responsible Use of Intellectual Property

Respect for intellectual property is essential in all research endeavors. Researchers should appropriately acknowledge and cite the work of others, including prior research, publications, and intellectual contributions. Plagiarism, unauthorized use of copyrighted materials, and improper attribution of ideas are strictly prohibited.

Animal Research and Ethical Treatment

If research involves the use of animals, researchers must adhere to the principles of ethical treatment and care of animals. All research involving animals should be conducted in accordance with applicable laws, regulations, and ethical guidelines for the welfare and humane treatment of animals.

Consequences of Research Ethics Violations

Violations of the ECE/CPE Research Ethics Policy may result in disciplinary actions, academic consequences, and damage to the reputation of individuals and the university. Consequences may include, but are not limited to:

  • Research Project Termination: Termination of the research project in question.
  • Academic Disciplinary Action: Subject to disciplinary actions as per university policies and guidelines.
  • Research Funding Revocation: Revocation of research funding and grants.
  • Loss of Research Privileges: Temporary or permanent loss of research privileges within the department or university.

Reporting Research Ethics Concerns

If you have concerns about potential research ethics violations or need guidance on ethical research practices, please report your concerns to the research ethics committee or the department head. We encourage an environment of open communication and transparency in addressing research ethics issues.

Conclusion

By adhering to the ECE/CPE Research Ethics Policy, we demonstrate our commitment to the responsible conduct of research and the promotion of ethical behavior in all research activities. Let us collectively work towards maintaining the highest standards of research integrity and upholding the values of academic excellence and ethical scholarship.
