ECE/CPE Academic Integrity Policy

Introduction

As students, faculty, and staff of the Electrical and Computer Engineering (ECE) and Computer Engineering (CPE) programs at De La Salle University, we uphold the principles of academic integrity and ethical conduct. Academic integrity is the foundation of our learning community, and it is essential to ensure a fair and just educational environment.

Policy Statement

ECE/CPE students are expected to maintain the highest standards of academic integrity throughout their studies. Academic integrity is demonstrated by honesty, trustworthiness, and ethical behavior in all academic activities, including coursework, examinations, research, and projects.

Examples of Academic Integrity Violations

Academic integrity violations include, but are not limited to:

  • Plagiarism: Presenting someone else's work, ideas, or intellectual property as one's own without proper acknowledgment.
  • Cheating: Using unauthorized materials, communication devices, or assistance during examinations or assignments.
  • Collusion: Working with others inappropriately to produce academic work that is meant to be individual effort.
  • Forgery: Falsifying or altering academic documents, grades, or records.
  • Unauthorized Collaboration: Collaborating on assignments or projects without permission when individual work is required.

Consequences of Academic Integrity Violations

Academic integrity violations are taken seriously at De La Salle University. Consequences may include, but are not limited to:

  • Grade Reduction: A failing grade for the assignment or examination in question.
  • Course Failure: Failing the course in which the violation occurred.
  • Academic Probation: Being placed on academic probation for repeated violations.
  • Academic Disciplinary Action: Subject to disciplinary actions as per university policies and guidelines.

Prevention and Support

The university provides resources and support to help students uphold academic integrity. It is essential to understand and practice proper citation and referencing methods. If you are uncertain about what constitutes academic integrity violations or need assistance in your studies, do not hesitate to seek guidance from faculty, academic advisors, or the university's academic support services.

Conclusion

Adhering to the ECE/CPE Academic Integrity Policy is fundamental to maintaining the credibility and quality of our educational programs. Let us work together to foster a culture of academic integrity and uphold the principles of honesty, responsibility, and ethical behavior in all aspects of our academic journey.
